📌 Abstract

Image geolocalization has traditionally been addressed through retrieval-based place recognition or geometry-based visual localization pipelines. Recent advances in Vision-Language Models (VLMs) have demonstrated strong zero-shot reasoning capabilities across multimodal tasks, yet their performance in geographic inference remains underexplored. In this work, we present a systematic benchmark of multiple state-of-the-art VLMs for country-level image geolocalization using ground-view imagery only. Instead of relying on image matching, GPS metadata, or task-specific training, we evaluate prompt-based country prediction in a zero-shot setting. The selected models are tested on three geographically diverse datasets to assess their robustness and generalization ability. Our results reveal substantial variation across models, highlighting the potential of semantic reasoning for coarse geolocalization and the limitations of current VLMs in capturing fine-grained geographic cues. This study establishes a strong foundation for future work at the intersection of multimodal reasoning and geographic understanding.

🚀 Evaluation Overview

Problem Setting

Given a single ground-view image, the model predicts the top 5 most likely countries in JSON format. The benchmark studies whether VLMs can infer location through semantic cues such as architecture, vegetation, road layout, and environmental context. 

{
  "predictions": ["Country1", "Country2", "Country3", "Country4", "Country5"]
}
Unconstrained evaluation Free-form country prediction without a predefined label list.
Label-constrained evaluation Country selection limited to the valid label space of the target dataset.
Multi-dimensional analysis Accuracy, environmental stratification, hop distance, and Geographic Error Reasonableness.

🎯 Key Contributions

Unified VLM Benchmark

A consistent evaluation setup for prompt-based geolocalization using multiple modern open VLM families.

GER: A New Error Metric

Geographic Error Reasonableness measures whether an incorrect prediction is still supported by local visual similarity.

Rich Failure Analysis

Detailed breakdowns across urban vs rural scenes, biome categories, and neighboring-country confusion patterns.

Main Findings

Qwen3-VL-4B leads overall It achieves the strongest balance of accuracy and error quality across the benchmark.
Urban scenes are easier Models perform better in environments with dense structural and semantic cues.
Error quality matters GER helps distinguish visually justified confusions from arbitrary geographic mistakes.

🔍 Why This Project Matters

Beyond Retrieval

This work studies semantic geolocalization directly, instead of relying on matching against a reference database.

Interpretable Failures

The analysis focuses not only on whether models fail, but also how and why they fail across geography and environment.

Foundation for Future Work

The benchmark provides a strong starting point for hybrid systems that combine reasoning, grounding, and retrieval.
